Who is Ghalib?

Ghalib (1797 - 1869) is a renowned poet of the Urdu and Persian languages. He was appointed the royal poet of the Mughal Empire in India. He is widely regarded as the pioneer of the Urdu Ghazal tradition. Abdul Rehman Bijanuri, a scholar of the Urdu literary tradition, wrote in 1921 that ‘India has two divinely inspired books: the Holy Vedas and the Divān-e-Ghalib’.

The Ghazal Tradition

The 'Ghazal' is a special kind of a poem. A ghazal consists of a chain of couplets. Each couplet on its own is considered a poem of its own - so the entire poem does not present a conitnuous narrative. What makes ghazals special is their metrical property of rythem that matches the 'Raag' and 'Taal' of classical music and dance. There are a range of meters that ghazals follow, each letter and pronounciation of each word matches precisely with the tenors of the chosen meter.

The Lover & the Beloved

The universal subject of the Ghazal World is the constant tugging and pulling between the lover and the beloved. Hidden deeper in this ebb and flow is a reflection of the profound issues that plague humanity - loneliness, poverty, curroption, good and evil.
